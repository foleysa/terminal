Hormuz Impact on Energy CostsANALYSIS
Direct Impact — Crude Oil
20% of global oil supply transits Hormuz. Closure has pushed WTI to $98.71/bbl and Brent to $103.14. Every energy-intensive manufacturer is seeing cost increases across transportation, heating, and process energy. This is structural — not a spike that corrects in weeks.
Indirect Impact — Natural Gas
US natural gas is largely domestic (shale), but global LNG demand is pulling on US export capacity. Henry Hub at $3.13/MMBtu is elevated but not crisis-level. Risk: if Hormuz extends through summer, LNG export demand could push domestic prices to $4-5/MMBtu — a 30-60% increase from today.
Downstream Impact — Electricity
40% of US electricity comes from natural gas. If gas prices spike, electricity rates follow with a 30-60 day lag. ERCOT (Texas), PJM (Mid-Atlantic), and ISO-NE (New England) are most exposed. Regions with high hydro/nuclear/renewable mix (Pacific NW, upper Midwest) are insulated.

Highest Risk Regions
ERCOT (Texas) and ISO-NE (New England) are most exposed to natural gas price spikes. Both regions are heavily gas-dependent for electricity generation with limited pipeline capacity. If Hormuz extends through summer peak demand, expect 15-25% electricity rate increases in these regions.
Lowest Risk Regions
BPA (Pacific Northwest) at 4.8¢/kWh is the cheapest industrial electricity in the US — hydro-powered and largely insulated from fossil fuel price swings. SPP (Central US) is also well-positioned with wind generation offsetting gas dependency. If you have location flexibility for new facilities, these regions offer structural energy cost advantages.

Deregulated vs Regulated Markets
In deregulated states (Texas, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Illinois, New York, Connecticut, and others), you can choose your electricity and gas supplier and negotiate rates. In regulated states, your utility is your only option — but you can still negotiate rate schedules, demand charges, and time-of-use structures. Know your market structure before negotiating.
Renewable PPA Opportunity
Corporate renewable PPAs (Power Purchase Agreements) lock in electricity pricing for 10-20 years at fixed rates, typically $0.03-0.06/kWh — well below current industrial rates. NextEra, Enel, and AES are the largest PPA counterparties. A PPA hedges your electricity cost permanently while also meeting ESG commitments.

IRA Opportunity
The Inflation Reduction Act's clean energy tax credits are the single largest energy cost reduction opportunity available today. A 1MW solar installation with the 30% ITC and accelerated depreciation can reduce facility electricity costs by 40-60% over 25 years with payback in 4-6 years. If you're not evaluating on-site solar or a renewable PPA, you're leaving money on the table.

Strategic Sourcing Actions — Energy & UtilitiesFSA INTELLIGENCE
URGENT — ACT THIS WEEK
Review every supplier contract with energy-linked pricing, fuel surcharges, or energy escalation clauses. Crude at $98.71/bbl means those clauses are activating NOW. You need to know your exposure before the surcharges hit — not after.
CRUDE · PASS-THROUGH · CONTRACTS
URGENT — ACT THIS WEEK
If you haven't hedged natural gas for Q2-Q3, the window is closing. Financial hedges (NYMEX futures/options) or physical fixed-price contracts from your gas supplier are both available. Henry Hub at $3.13 is elevated but could go to $4-5 if Hormuz extends through summer. Lock what you can.
NAT GAS · HEDGING · FUTURES
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
In deregulated states, run a competitive solicitation for electricity and gas supply. Current contracts signed 12+ months ago are likely below market — but new competitive bids may still beat your incumbent. Get 3-5 quotes from retail energy suppliers. Constellation, Direct Energy/NRG, and Shell Energy all compete aggressively for industrial load.
ELECTRICITY · GAS · DEREGULATED MARKETS
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Evaluate a corporate renewable PPA. Fixed electricity at $0.03-0.06/kWh for 10-20 years is the most powerful energy hedge available. IRA tax credits make the economics better than ever. Even if you don't sign today, get indicative pricing from NextEra, Enel, or AES so you have the data when leadership asks.
RENEWABLE · PPA · HEDGING
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Evaluate on-site solar + storage for facilities with large rooftops or adjacent land. 30% ITC plus accelerated depreciation creates 4-6 year payback. This permanently reduces your grid dependency and insulates you from future energy price spikes. The IRA credits are available now — they may not be forever.
SOLAR · IRA · ON-SITE GENERATION
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
For facilities in ERCOT (Texas) or ISO-NE (New England) — the two highest-risk regions — develop an energy contingency plan. Identify backup fuel options (propane, diesel gen), evaluate demand response programs (get paid to curtail during peaks), and review your interruptible vs firm rate structure.
GRID RISK · CONTINGENCY · DEMAND RESPONSE
MONITOR
Watch EPA's power plant emissions rule implementation. Accelerated coal plant retirements will tighten grid capacity in the Southeast and Midwest over the next 2-3 years. If your facilities are in these regions, plan for gradually increasing electricity costs and evaluate backup generation or energy storage.
