Why This Category Matters
The Unmanaged Spend Problem
Janitorial and facilities is the category most likely to have ZERO strategic sourcing attention. Every plant, office, and warehouse buys tissue paper, trash bags, hand soap, and cleaning chemicals — but nobody negotiates them. The result: each facility buys from whoever shows up (local janitorial distributor, Staples, Amazon, Costco), at whatever price they quote, with no contract and no volume leverage. Typical overspend: 15-40% vs. what a GPO or consolidated contract would deliver.
The Opportunity
This is the easiest category to save money in because nobody has tried. A single GPO membership (OMNIA Partners, Vizient/Provista) or a consolidated contract with a national distributor (HD Supply, Staples Business Advantage, Cintas) typically saves 15-25% across the entire category. On $500K annual Jan/San spend, that's $75K-125K/year — for a procurement initiative that takes 2-4 weeks to implement.
Sub-Services Breakdown
CLEANING CHEMICALS — 20%
Floor care, disinfectants, degreasers, glass cleaners. Concentrated products diluted on-site save 30-50% vs ready-to-use. Ecolab and Diversey dominate the dispensing systems.
RESTROOM SUPPLIES — 25%
Paper towels, tissue, hand soap, dispensers. Dispenser lock-in is real — proprietary dispensers only accept the manufacturer's product. Georgia-Pacific (Enmotion), Kimberly-Clark (Scott), and SCA (Tork) all use this model.
WASTE HAULING — 20%
Dumpster rental, scheduled pickups, recycling. Waste Management and Republic Services control 60%+ of US market. Auto-renewal contracts with annual escalation clauses. Always rebid at renewal.
PEST CONTROL — 10%
Monthly/quarterly service. Rollins (Orkin, HomeTeam) and Rentokil (Terminix) dominate. Bundled with Ecolab in many facilities. Competitive bid every 3 years — pricing softens 10-15% under competition.
CONTRACT CLEANING — 15%
Outsourced janitorial. ABM, Marsden, C&W Services. High turnover workforce, quality inconsistency. SLA-based contracts with penalty clauses for missed service are essential.
LANDSCAPING / SNOW — 10%
Seasonal services. BrightView dominates commercial landscaping. Snow removal contracts should be fixed-price, not per-event — heavy snow years destroy per-push budgets.

ECOLAB (NYSE: ECL)
Cleaning chemicals, dispensing systems, pest control, food safety. The 800-lb gorilla in facilities services. If you use Ecolab for cleaning AND Nalco for water AND Ecolab pest — map your total exposure. Significant leverage opportunity.
Services
WASTE MANAGEMENT (NYSE: WM)
$20B+. Dominates US waste hauling. Auto-renewal contracts with 3-5% annual escalators. ALWAYS rebid before auto-renewal. WM's best pricing comes when they think they're losing the account.
REPUBLIC SERVICES (NYSE: RSG)
$14.9B. #2 in US waste. Use as competitive alternative to WM. In many markets, WM and Republic are the only two options — which limits leverage. Add a regional hauler as a third bid when possible.

$3.1B. Owns Orkin, HomeTeam Pest Defense, Waltham, Western Pest. If you use Orkin at some facilities and HomeTeam at others — same company. Consolidate for multi-site pricing.

$9.6B. Uniforms, facility services, restroom supplies, first aid, fire protection. Cintas bundles everything — and the bundle creates lock-in. Unbundle and bid restroom supplies separately from uniforms for better pricing.

A mid-size manufacturer could easily have $500K-1M+ in total Ecolab spend across 4-5 different service lines — each managed by a different internal team with no cross-visibility. Map it. Managed Services vs. Self-Managed — The DecisionFSA ANALYSIS
GPO / National Contract
BEST FOR: Multi-Location, $250K+ Spend
GPO membership (OMNIA Partners, Vizient/Provista) provides pre-negotiated national contracts with HD Supply, Staples Business Advantage, Georgia-Pacific, Kimberly-Clark, Waste Management, Cintas, and others. No membership fee. Savings: 15-25% vs. unmanaged spot buying. Implementation: 2-4 weeks (sign enrollment forms, route orders through GPO catalog).
SAVINGS MATH
$500K annual Jan/San spend × 20% GPO savings = $100K/year. Time to implement: 10-20 hours of procurement effort. ROI: $5,000-10,000 per hour invested. This is the highest-ROI procurement initiative in most organizations — and the one nobody does because "it's just toilet paper and trash bags."
Integrated Facilities Management (IFM)
BEST FOR: Large Campus, Complex Needs
Outsource the entire facilities function to a single provider (CBRE, JLL, Cushman & Wakefield, ABM). They manage cleaning, maintenance, landscaping, pest, waste, mail, reception — everything. One contract, one SLA, one invoice. Trade unit-cost savings for management simplicity. Makes sense when facilities management isn't a core competency and internal team is spread thin.
WATCH OUT
IFM providers make margin by subcontracting services. You're paying their management fee (8-12%) on top of the subcontractor cost. For individual services (waste, pest, cleaning), you can often get better pricing by contracting directly. IFM is a convenience play, not a cost play. Use it when you need it, not as a default.
Dispenser Lock-In — The Hidden Cost of "Free" Equipment
GP, KC, and Essity all offer "free" dispenser installation. The catch: proprietary dispensers only accept the manufacturer's paper products. You save $2,000 in dispenser costs and lock yourself into 5+ years of above-market paper product pricing. If you're planning a restroom refresh, specify Tork or universal-compatible dispensers that accept multiple brands. Or negotiate the paper product pricing BEFORE accepting the "free" dispensers.

Strategic Sourcing Actions — Janitorial & FacilitiesFSA INTELLIGENCE
URGENT — EASIEST WIN IN PROCUREMENT
Join a GPO for Jan/San supplies. OMNIA Partners or Vizient/Provista. No membership fee. Sign up, route your Jan/San purchases through their pre-negotiated contracts, and save 15-25% immediately. On $500K annual spend, that's $75K-125K/year for 10 hours of work. Do this today.
GPO · JAN/SAN · IMMEDIATE SAVINGS
URGENT — ACT THIS WEEK
Check your Waste Management and Republic Services contract auto-renewal dates. These contracts auto-renew with 3-5% annual escalators. File a termination notice 90 days before renewal even if you plan to stay — it opens the negotiation. Get 3 bids: WM, Republic, and one regional hauler. Typical savings on rebid: 10-20%.
WASTE · AUTO-RENEWAL · REBID
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Map your total Ecolab spend: Nalco Water (boiler/cooling), cleaning chemicals (jan/san), pest control, food safety, Purolite (resin). Each service is often managed by a different person with no cross-visibility. Consolidate the negotiation. Total Ecolab spend of $500K+ gives you leverage no single service line has.
ECOLAB · CROSS-CATEGORY · LEVERAGE
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Audit your restroom dispenser fleet. If all dispensers are proprietary (Enmotion, Scott, etc.), you're locked into one paper product supplier. For the next dispenser refresh, specify universal-compatible models or negotiate paper product pricing as a condition of accepting new dispensers. Never accept "free" dispensers without a paper contract.
DISPENSER · LOCK-IN · PAPER
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Switch from ready-to-use cleaning chemicals to concentrated products with proportioning dispensers. Concentrated chemicals diluted on-site cost 30-50% less per use than pre-mixed. Reduce freight (shipping water is expensive), reduce storage space, and improve consistency (proportioner delivers exact dilution every time vs. employees guessing).
CONCENTRATED · CHEMICALS · SAVINGS
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
For outsourced janitorial contracts: implement SLA-based agreements with inspection scoring and penalty clauses. ABM, Marsden, and C&W all accept this structure. Monthly inspections with a standardized scorecard (1-5 rating per area), penalty triggers below 3.5 average, and quarterly business reviews. This is how you get consistent quality from a high-turnover workforce.
SLA · CLEANING · QUALITY
MONITOR
Waste hauling costs will increase as landfill capacity tightens and recycling markets fluctuate. China's National Sword policy permanently disrupted recycling economics — contaminated recyclables now go to landfill, increasing disposal costs. Monitor your hauler's recycling revenue share (if any) and verify that recyclables are actually being recycled, not landfilled at recycling rates.
