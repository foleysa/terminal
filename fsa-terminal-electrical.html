Key DependenciesANALYSIS
Copper — Supply Squeeze
Copper is 28% of electrical component cost. LME inventories at 15-year low. Chilean mine output down 12%. Every cable, busbar, transformer, and motor winding is affected. This is the single biggest cost driver in the category right now.
Semiconductors — Capacity Improving But Fragile
The 2021-2023 chip shortage has largely resolved for mature-node chips (MCUs, power semiconductors). But leading-edge capacity is concentrated in Taiwan (TSMC) and South Korea (Samsung). Any Taiwan Strait escalation would be catastrophic for the category. Monitor geopolitical risk.
Plastics & Insulation — Hormuz Exposure
PVC, polyethylene, and specialty insulation materials are petrochemical-derived. Crude at $98.71/bbl is pushing feedstock costs up 6-8%. Wire and cable insulation costs will follow with a 30-60 day lag.

⚠ Concentration Risk — Eaton / Cooper
Eaton acquired Cooper Industries in 2012 for $11.8B. If you source circuit breakers from Cooper and switchgear from Eaton, you have one supplier. Cooper's B-Line (cable trays) is also Eaton. Map your full Eaton/Cooper exposure across all electrical subcategories.
⚠ Hidden Exposure — Schneider / Square D / APC
Schneider Electric owns Square D, APC, and ASCO. These brands are often purchased by different teams within the same company — facilities buys APC (UPS), electrical buys Square D (panels), maintenance buys ASCO (transfer switches). All one parent company, one balance sheet.

Section 301 — Broadest Impact in Any Category
Nearly every electrical HS code from China faces 25% Section 301 on top of MFN duty. Lithium-ion batteries are escalating from 25% to 100%. USTR is reviewing expansion to industrial automation components (PLCs, servos, robotics) — if enacted, that's another 25% on automation capex sourced from China.
Alternative Sourcing Advantage
Japan (Mitsubishi, Omron, Yaskawa) and Germany (Siemens, Phoenix Contact, Wago) produce equivalent PLCs, servos, and automation components at 2.7% MFN duty — a 22% cost advantage over Chinese-origin equivalents. Mexico-origin wire and cable enters at 0% under USMCA. The tariff math makes reshoring and friend-shoring the obvious move.
BIS Entity List — Export Control Risk
The Bureau of Industry and Security maintains the Entity List restricting technology exports to certain Chinese companies. If your Chinese electrical suppliers are on or near the Entity List, you face dual risk: they can't get the components they need to build your products AND you may need a license to buy from them. Check BIS regularly.
Source: USTR HTS, Section 301 Lists 1-4, BIS Entity List, CBP ACE Portal

Overall Category Health: Strong
The major electrical component suppliers are financially healthy across the board. This is a mature, oligopolistic market with high barriers to entry. The risk in this category is not supplier insolvency — it's tariff exposure, copper price volatility, and geographic concentration in China. Financial risk is low. Trade policy risk is extreme.

COPPER · WIRE · PRICING · SUPPLY
URGENT — ACT THIS WEEK
Lithium-ion battery tariff escalating from 25% to 100% on Chinese-origin cells and packs. If you source batteries or battery-powered equipment from China, secure supply at current tariff rates or shift to non-Chinese sources (LG Chem/South Korea, Panasonic/Japan, domestic AESC/Tesla). The 100% tariff will make Chinese cells uneconomic.
BATTERIES · TARIFF · 301 ESCALATION
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Shift PLC and automation sourcing from China to Japan (Mitsubishi, Omron, Keyence) or Germany (Siemens, Beckhoff, Phoenix Contact). The MFN duty is 2.7% vs 27.7% for Chinese-origin. That's a 25-point tariff advantage — and the quality is often superior. Run the total cost comparison including tariff, lead time, and support availability.
AUTOMATION · PLC · TARIFF ARBITRAGE
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Map your Eaton/Cooper and Schneider/Square D/APC exposure across all electrical subcategories. Most companies discover these hidden concentration risks only when they do an Exhibit 21 audit. If you buy from 3 brands that are all Schneider subsidiaries, you have one supplier — not three.
CONCENTRATION RISK · EXHIBIT 21
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Source wire and cable from Mexico under USMCA at 0% duty. Southwire, Encore Wire, and Prysmian all have Mexico manufacturing. If you're buying Chinese-origin cable at 28.5% combined duty, the Mexico switch is a no-brainer on total landed cost alone.
WIRE · CABLE · USMCA · NEARSHORE
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Check the BIS Entity List quarterly against your Chinese electrical suppliers. If a supplier lands on the Entity List, you face immediate supply disruption AND potential compliance liability. Don't find out from the news — build a quarterly screen into your supplier management process.
COMPLIANCE · BIS · EXPORT CONTROLS
MONITOR
TSMC and Samsung dominate leading-edge semiconductor production. Any Taiwan Strait escalation would disrupt the entire electronics supply chain — not just chips, but every product that contains a chip. This is a low-probability, catastrophic-impact scenario. Don't plan for it tactically — but make sure you have 90-day buffer stock on critical semiconductor-dependent components.
