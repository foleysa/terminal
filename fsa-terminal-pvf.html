Chinese Butt-Weld Fittings — Up to 165% Combined Duty
Chinese-origin butt-weld carbon steel fittings face 25% Section 232 + 25% Section 301 + 56-140% antidumping duty. Total combined duty can reach 165%. There is no scenario where Chinese fittings are cost-competitive. Source domestically, from Italy (no AD), or USMCA-qualifying Canadian/Mexican mills.
AD/CVD Active Orders — PVF Category
Active antidumping and countervailing duty orders exist on welded pipe from: China, South Korea, India, Turkey, Vietnam, Oman, Pakistan, UAE, Thailand, and others. ITC sunset reviews ongoing. Check the current AD/CVD rate for your specific supplier and country of origin BEFORE placing orders — rates change with annual administrative reviews.
USMCA Exemption — Verify Rules of Origin
USMCA-qualifying pipe and fittings from Canada and Mexico may be exempt from Section 232, but rules of origin requirements are strict. The steel must be melted AND poured in a USMCA country. Simply fabricating pipe from imported steel doesn't qualify. Verify with your customs broker on every shipment.
Source: USTR, ITC AD/CVD Orders, Section 232 Proclamations, CBP ACE Portal
Buy America / Build America Buy America (BABA)OMB · FEDERAL REGISTER
What Is BABA?
The Build America, Buy America Act (BABA) — part of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) — requires that iron, steel, manufactured products, and construction materials used in federally funded infrastructure projects be produced in the United States. This is the most significant domestic sourcing mandate in decades and directly affects every PVF purchase on a federal project.
BABA Requirements by Material Type
MaterialBABA RequirementWhat It Means
Iron & Steel (pipe, fittings, flanges)
ALL manufacturing processes in US
Melted, poured, AND fabricated domestically. Imported steel = non-compliant.
Manufactured Products (valves, actuators)
55%+ domestic content by cost
Final assembly in US. >55% of component cost from domestic sources.
Construction Materials (gaskets, sealants)
ALL manufacturing in US
All manufacturing steps performed domestically. Varies by material type.
Common BABA Compliance Failures in PVF
The #1 failure: pipe that's fabricated in the US from imported steel. Under BABA, the steel must be melted AND poured in the US — not just rolled or welded here. The #2 failure: valves assembled in the US from imported castings. If the casting was done overseas, the 55% domestic content threshold gets very tight. Audit your supply chain before you submit a bid.
Waiver Process
BABA waivers exist for: (1) non-availability — the product isn't made in the US, (2) unreasonable cost — domestic product is 25%+ more expensive, (3) public interest. Waivers are granted by the funding agency and published in the Federal Register. The process takes 30-90 days. Don't count on a waiver for standard PVF products — domestic supply exists.
BABA-Ready Suppliers
Nucor Tubular, Tenaris (Bay City, TX mill), Mueller Water, and IPSCO (now Evraz NA) all produce BABA-compliant pipe with US-melted, US-poured steel. For valves: Flowserve, Emerson (Fisher), and Cameron all have US manufacturing — but verify domestic content percentage on a per-product basis. MRC Global and Ferguson can document BABA compliance through their supply chain traceability programs.
Source: OMB Guidance M-22-11, IIJA §70914, Federal Register waiver notices

BABA · MATRIX · COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Stainless steel PVF is driven by nickel pricing (+6.2% YTD). If you have high stainless volume, evaluate duplex grades (2205, 2507) which use less nickel than 316L but offer superior corrosion resistance. The nickel savings on duplex can offset the higher fabrication cost — and the trend is moving in duplex's favor as nickel rises.
