Resin Pricing & Index Mechanisms — Managing the VolatilityFSA ANALYSIS · ICIS · CDI
The Feedstock Chain: Crude → NGL → Monomer → Resin → Your Part
Crude oil → cracking → ethylene (PE feedstock) and propylene (PP feedstock). Natural gas → ethane → ethylene. Every step has a margin, a processing cost, and market dynamics. Your resin price is the end of a 4-5 step chain that starts with crude oil and natural gas. Understanding the chain is the foundation for every pricing negotiation. If your supplier says "costs are up" — ask which feedstock, how much, and show them the published index to verify.
Published Price Indices
CDI (CHEMICAL DATA INC) — US STANDARD
CDI publishes monthly commodity resin prices (PE, PP, PS, PVC) based on transaction data from producers, distributors, and converters. CDI is the most widely used contract index for US resin pricing. Your contract should reference "CDI monthly" for the applicable resin grade. CDI data is subscription-based ($3K-5K/year) but your supplier should provide the applicable index value with every price adjustment.
ICIS — GLOBAL STANDARD
ICIS publishes weekly/monthly pricing for all major resins and chemicals globally. More granular than CDI but more expensive ($5K-15K/year depending on scope). For companies spending $5M+ on resins, ICIS subscription pays for itself in one negotiation. ICIS also publishes capacity, supply/demand analysis, and margin data that quantifies producer profitability — leverage data.
FRED / BLS PPI — FREE
BLS PPI series WPU071 (Plastic Resins and Materials) is available free on FRED. Less granular than CDI/ICIS (aggregate, not by resin type) but useful for trend validation and contract escalation clauses. Your BLS Price Validator tool in this terminal can track this index. Use it to verify that your supplier's claimed increases match the published BLS trend.
Contract Pricing Structures
FORMULA PRICING — BEST PRACTICE
Resin cost = Published index (CDI/ICIS) + Fixed conversion fee. The conversion fee covers the compounder/molder's processing, overhead, and margin — this is fixed and negotiated once. The resin cost floats with the published index — transparent, verifiable, and fair to both parties. This is the gold standard for resin procurement. If your supplier will not agree to formula pricing, they are hiding margin in the resin pass-through.
QUARTERLY ADJUSTMENT WITH CAP
Price adjusts quarterly based on published index movement, capped at ±5% per quarter. Smooths volatility for both parties. The cap protects you from spikes and protects the supplier from crashes. Fair structure for moderate-volume buyers who cannot handle monthly index swings in their pricing models.
FIXED PRICE WITH BAND
Price fixed for 6-12 months as long as the resin index stays within ±10% of the base. If the index moves outside the band, pricing reopens. Best for: budgeting certainty on stable categories. Risk: if crude spikes 30%, your supplier will invoke the reopener. But it prevents nickel-and-dime monthly adjustments on small movements.
Never Accept "Market-Based" Pricing
"Market-based" or "prevailing market" pricing means the supplier sets the price with no published reference. You have no way to verify the increase. On a $2M annual resin spend, a supplier charging 3% above the actual market index costs you $60K/year in hidden margin. Always tie pricing to a published index (CDI, ICIS, or BLS PPI at minimum).
Source: CDI, ICIS, BLS PPI WPU071, FRED, Plastics News, industry pricing practices

US Is a Net Exporter of Commodity Resins
The US is one of the world's largest PE and PP producers (Gulf Coast ethane cracker advantage). Commodity resin tariffs are largely irrelevant because domestic supply exceeds demand — the US exports PE and PP. Tariff exposure is concentrated in engineering plastics (nylon, polycarbonate, specialty compounds) sourced from Asia, where Section 301 adds 25% on Chinese-origin material. For commodity PE/PP, the cost driver is crude/NGL pricing, not trade policy.
AD/CVD — PET Resin From China
Antidumping and countervailing duties are active on PET resin from China, India, and other countries. Combined rates can reach 40-80% depending on the exporter. If you buy PET for packaging or thermoforming, verify country of origin. US producers (Indorama Ventures, DAK Americas, Far Eastern) supply the domestic market competitively without AD/CVD exposure.

Strategic Sourcing Actions — Plastics & RubberFSA INTELLIGENCE
URGENT — INDEX YOUR CONTRACTS
If any resin contract uses "market-based" or "quarterly negotiation" pricing without a published index reference, fix it now. Move to formula pricing: published CDI or ICIS index + fixed conversion fee. On $2M annual resin spend, a supplier charging 3% above published market costs $60K/year in hidden margin. Index-based pricing makes every penny verifiable.
INDEX · CDI · ICIS · FORMULA PRICING
URGENT — VERIFY COMPOUNDER PASS-THROUGH
If you buy custom compounds, request the resin index your compounder uses for pass-through pricing. Verify it matches CDI or ICIS published data — not a "proprietary" index. Pull the last 12 months of pass-through adjustments and compare to published index movement. Any consistent gap above 1-2% is margin padding. Call it out with data.
COMPOUNDER · PASS-THROUGH · AUDIT
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Negotiate quarterly adjustment caps (±5% per quarter) on your largest resin contracts. Caps smooth volatility for budgeting without eliminating the supplier's ability to pass through legitimate cost changes. A 5% quarterly cap limits your annual exposure to ±20% while still allowing the market to clear. Both sides benefit from predictability.
CAP · QUARTERLY · VOLATILITY
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
If you consume 1M+ lbs/year of any single resin grade, get a direct allocation from the producer (Dow, LyondellBasell, ExxonMobil, INEOS) rather than buying through a distributor. Direct pricing is typically 5-10% below distributor pricing. At 1M lbs of PE at $0.80/lb, that is $40K-80K in savings. Producers allocate directly to customers above volume thresholds.
DIRECT · PRODUCER · VOLUME
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Evaluate post-consumer recycled (PCR) resin for non-critical applications. PCR PE and PP trade at 0-15% premium over virgin depending on grade and availability. Sustainability mandates (California SB 54, EU Packaging Regulation) are creating demand pull for PCR. Locking in PCR supply now — before mandates tighten — secures availability and positions you ahead of competitors scrambling to comply later.
PCR · RECYCLED · SUSTAINABILITY
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
For natural rubber users: hedge with SICOM (Singapore Commodity Exchange) futures or physical forward contracts through a rubber broker (Halcyon Agri, R1 International). Natural rubber is weather-dependent (90% from Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia) and subject to supply shocks from monsoons, disease (leaf blight), and export restrictions. A 3-6 month forward position smooths procurement cost.
NATURAL RUBBER · HEDGE · FORWARD
MONITOR
New Gulf Coast PE/PP capacity (ExxonMobil Baytown expansion, Shell Monaca PA, Formosa Sunshine LA) coming online 2026-2027. When these crackers reach full production, US PE/PP supply increases significantly. Watch ICIS capacity utilization — when it drops below 85%, pricing power shifts to buyers. Time your next contract renewal to coincide with capacity additions for maximum leverage.
