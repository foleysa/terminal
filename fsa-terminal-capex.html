Key DependenciesANALYSIS
Steel — 30% of Equipment Cost
Every machine tool, press, and furnace is steel-intensive. Section 232 adds 25% tariff on ALL steel imports regardless of origin. Domestic steel HRC up 18% YTD. An OEM quoting a $500K press today is baking in steel at current prices. If steel continues rising, expect requotes on anything ordered more than 90 days ago.
Controls = Automation Lock-In
Every major piece of capital equipment comes with an integrated control system — and that control system locks you into an automation ecosystem. A press with a Siemens CNC means Siemens drives, Siemens HMI, Siemens programming tools. Specify the control platform at the time of equipment purchase — not after. This is the most expensive decision inside the equipment buy.
Freight & Rigging — Don't Forget
A 40,000-lb CNC machine costs $15K-40K to ship and rig into place. Specialized hauling (oversize/overweight permits), crane rental, and millwright labor for installation are frequently excluded from the OEM quote. Budget 5-8% of equipment cost for freight, rigging, and installation — and get it quoted before you sign the PO.

Buy vs Lease vs Used — The Acquisition DecisionFSA ANALYSIS · IRS
Why This Tab Exists
The acquisition structure changes the economics more than the equipment price itself. A $1M machine bought new with 80% bonus depreciation, leased over 5 years, or purchased used at $450K from an auction — these are three completely different financial outcomes. Most procurement teams default to "buy new from OEM" without evaluating the alternatives. That default costs money.
Option 1 — Buy New
WHEN IT MAKES SENSE
Latest technology/precision required. OEM warranty (typically 1-2 years). Access to OEM training and support. Eligible for Section 179 and bonus depreciation. Long useful life (15-25 years for quality machine tools). Custom configuration for your specific application.
WHEN IT DOESN'T
6-18 month lead time may be unacceptable. Full price at current steel/material cost peak. Technology may be superseded in 3-5 years (CNC controls evolve fast). Depreciation schedule may not match actual production need.
Option 2 — Buy Used / Rebuilt
WHEN IT MAKES SENSE
40-60% of new price for comparable capability. Immediate availability (no 12-month wait). Still eligible for Section 179 / bonus depreciation. Machine tool auction market (Hilco, Heritage, Maynards) has excellent inventory post-COVID. A 10-year-old Mazak CNC that was well-maintained is still a highly capable machine.
WHEN IT DOESN'T
No OEM warranty (buy from a dealer who offers 90-day warranty). Older controls may be obsolete (parts availability). Unknown maintenance history (get a third-party inspection before purchase — $2K-5K well spent). May not meet latest precision tolerances for tight-tolerance work.
Option 3 — Lease / Finance
WHEN IT MAKES SENSE
Preserve cash for operations. Technology will be obsolete in 5-7 years (upgrade at lease end). Operating lease keeps asset off balance sheet (depending on ASC 842 treatment). Monthly payment may be more predictable than lump-sum capex approval. Some OEMs offer favorable financing (0-2% for 36 months).
WHEN IT DOESN'T
Total cost of lease always exceeds purchase price (you're paying for the capital). Interest rates at current levels (6-8%) make leasing expensive. You don't get the Section 179 / bonus depreciation benefit on an operating lease (you do on a capital lease / finance lease). At end of lease, you own nothing unless you exercise a buyout option.
Tax Incentives — 2026
Section 179 — $1.22M Limit (2026)
Section 179 allows you to expense up to $1.22M (2026 limit) of qualifying equipment in the year it's placed in service instead of depreciating over its useful life. Applies to new AND used equipment. Phase-out begins at $3.05M total equipment purchases.
Bonus Depreciation — 80% in 2026
For equipment exceeding the Section 179 limit, bonus depreciation allows 80% first-year write-off (2026), declining to 60% (2027), 40% (2028), 20% (2029), 0% (2030). A $2M machine placed in service in 2026 gets $1.6M first-year depreciation. That's real cash flow impact on your tax bill. Applies to new AND used equipment (must be new to you).
Bonus Depreciation Is Declining
2025: 100% → 2026: 80% → 2027: 60% → 2028: 40% → 2029: 20% → 2030: 0%. Every year you wait, the tax benefit shrinks by 20 points. If you're going to buy capital equipment in the next 2-3 years, there's a strong tax case for doing it in 2026 at 80%.
Source: IRS Section 179, TCJA bonus depreciation schedule, ASC 842 lease accounting

Machine Tools — Japan/Germany vs China
The world's premium machine tool manufacturers (DMG MORI, Mazak, Okuma, TRUMPF, Grob) are Japanese and German. They enter at 2.4-4.4% MFN duty. Chinese machine tools (Shenyang, Dalian, Haitian) face 25% Section 301 on top of MFN. For precision CNC work, the quality gap between Japanese/German and Chinese machines is significant. The tariff widens an already unfavorable cost-quality equation for Chinese equipment on critical applications.
Haas — Made in USA
Haas Automation (Oxnard, CA) is the largest US-based CNC machine tool manufacturer. No import duty, no tariff exposure, 100% domestic. For applications where Haas capability meets your precision requirements, it's the most tariff-protected option. Haas pricing is typically 30-50% below DMG MORI and Mazak — the trade-off is precision tolerance and build quality on the highest-end work.

Strategic Sourcing Actions — Capital EquipmentFSA INTELLIGENCE
URGENT — ACT THIS WEEK
If you have 2027 production capacity needs, place capital equipment POs now. Custom CNC, presses, and furnaces are 9-18 month lead times. Every month of delay is a month your new capacity isn't producing revenue. The lead time is the constraint — not the price.
LEAD TIME · CAPACITY · 2027 PLANNING
TAX — ACT BEFORE DECEMBER 31
Bonus depreciation is 80% in 2026, dropping to 60% in 2027. On a $1M equipment purchase, that's a $200K difference in first-year depreciation. Any equipment that can be placed in service before December 31, 2026 should be — the tax math demands it. Work backward from December 31 to determine your latest PO date given lead times.
BONUS DEPRECIATION · TAX · TIMING
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Before buying new, check the used equipment market. Hilco Industrial, Heritage Global, Maynards, and Perfection Industrial Sales all have active auction inventory. A 5-10 year old Mazak or DMG MORI CNC at 40-60% of new price with a dealer warranty is often the better financial decision — and it's available in weeks, not months.
USED EQUIPMENT · AUCTION · SAVINGS
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Get freight, rigging, and installation quoted BEFORE signing the equipment PO. A $500K machine with $40K in freight/rigging and $25K in installation is really a $565K purchase. These costs are frequently excluded from OEM quotes and surprise the buyer after the PO is signed. Get total installed cost upfront.
TOTAL INSTALLED COST · FREIGHT · RIGGING
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Specify the automation/control platform at the time of equipment purchase. A press with a FANUC CNC vs Siemens CNC is a 15-20 year ecosystem decision. Align with your plant's existing automation standard to avoid maintaining two parallel spare parts inventories and two sets of trained technicians.
CONTROLS · AUTOMATION · LOCK-IN
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Evaluate forklift fleet: own vs lease vs full-service lease. Toyota, Crown, and Hyster-Yale all offer full-service leases that include maintenance, tires, and battery/charger. For a 50-unit fleet, full-service lease often delivers lower total cost than ownership because the OEM's maintenance is more efficient and they replace units before major failures.
FORKLIFT · FLEET · LEASE vs OWN
MONITOR
AMT (Association for Manufacturing Technology) publishes monthly US machine tool orders data. This is the leading indicator for capital equipment pricing and lead times. When orders are high, lead times extend and OEMs have less pricing flexibility. When orders soften, negotiating leverage increases. Track the AMT USMTO report quarterly.
