⚠ Smurfit WestRock Merger — Market Power
Smurfit Kappa and WestRock merged in July 2024, creating the world's largest listed corrugated packaging company at $34B revenue. If you bought from both WestRock and Smurfit Kappa pre-merger, you now have one supplier. Combined entity has significant pricing power in North American containerboard.
⚠ Sealed Air — Activist Pressure
Sealed Air is under activist investor pressure. Potential strategic actions include spinning off the food packaging (Cryovac) business from protective packaging (Bubble Wrap). If Sealed Air is a significant supplier, monitor for restructuring announcements that could disrupt supply or change your commercial terms.
⚠ Uline — Premium Pricing, Fast Delivery
Uline is the largest privately held packaging distributor in North America. Their value proposition is same-day shipping from 13 US warehouses. But you pay for it — Uline pricing is typically 15-30% above contract pricing from manufacturers or competitive distributors. Use Uline for emergency/rush orders only. Commodity packaging should be on contract with a manufacturer or competitive distributor.

Sustainability & Regulatory MandatesEPA · STATE LAW · EU
Why This Matters for Procurement
Packaging sustainability is no longer a CSR initiative — it's a compliance requirement. Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) laws, plastic bans, recycled content mandates, and customer sustainability requirements are rewriting the packaging specification for every industrial shipper. The cost of ignoring this isn't a PR problem — it's a regulatory fine and a lost customer.
Active Regulations
EPR — EXTENDED PRODUCER RESPONSIBILITY
California (SB 54): All packaging must be recyclable or compostable by 2032. 65% source reduction for single-use plastics by 2032. Producers fund collection/recycling infrastructure.
Colorado, Oregon, Maine, Minnesota have passed similar EPR laws. More states following.
RECYCLED CONTENT MANDATES
California (AB 793): Plastic beverage containers must contain 15% PCR by 2022, 25% by 2025, 50% by 2030. Other states expanding to non-beverage packaging.
New Jersey, Washington have their own recycled content requirements.
EPS FOAM BANS
Expanded polystyrene (EPS/Styrofoam) foam packaging banned in 8+ states and dozens of municipalities. If you ship to California, New York, Maine, Maryland, Virginia, Vermont, Colorado, or Washington — your protective packaging cannot be EPS foam.
Material Transitions
EPS FOAM → MOLDED PULP / PAPER
Molded fiber (pulp) packaging replaces EPS foam for protective cushioning. Cost premium: 10-20% vs EPS but avoids regulatory bans and meets customer sustainability requirements. Sonoco, Sealed Air, and Pregis all offer alternatives.
PLASTIC FILM → PAPER-BASED ALTERNATIVES
Paper-based stretch wrap, paper void fill, and paper mailer bags replacing plastic equivalents. Performance gap is closing. Cost premium: 15-30% but declining as scale increases. Ranpak (NYSE: PACK) is the market leader in paper-based void fill and cushioning.
VIRGIN → POST-CONSUMER RECYCLED (PCR)
PCR content in plastic packaging is increasingly required by regulation AND by customers (Walmart, Amazon, Target all have packaging sustainability scorecards). PCR resin currently trades at a 10-25% premium over virgin — but the premium may decline as recycling infrastructure scales.
RIGHT-SIZING
Before switching materials, reduce packaging volume. Most companies over-package by 20-40%. Right-sizing (matching box dimensions to product) reduces material consumption, reduces freight cost (dimensional weight pricing), and improves sustainability scores simultaneously. No material premium required.
Source: California SB 54, AB 793, state EPR legislation, EU Packaging & Packaging Waste Regulation

Low Tariff Category Overall
Most packaging is produced domestically or in USMCA countries. The tariff exposure is concentrated in Chinese-origin poly bags, plastic containers, and stretch film. Corrugated boxes are overwhelmingly produced domestically (International Paper, Smurfit WestRock, PCA all have US mills). Pallets are nearly 100% domestic. The real cost driver is crude oil (resin) and OCC (corrugated), not tariffs.
ISPM-15 — Export Pallet Requirement
All wood packaging materials (pallets, crates, dunnage) used in international trade must be heat-treated or fumigated per ISPM-15 and stamped with the certified mark. Non-compliant pallets are rejected at port. If you export, verify your pallet supplier is ISPM-15 certified. Violations result in shipment rejection, fumigation at port ($500-2,000), and potential quarantine.
Source: USTR HTS, ITC DataWeb, ISPM-15 (IPPC)

This turns an unpredictable pass-through into a structured, auditable adjustment you can verify with FRED data.
RESIN · INDEX CLAUSE · STRETCH FILM
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Run a packaging right-sizing analysis on your top 20 SKUs by shipping volume. Most companies over-package by 20-40%. Right-sizing reduces material cost, reduces dimensional weight freight charges, and improves your sustainability metrics — three wins from one initiative. No material substitution required.
RIGHT-SIZING · MATERIAL · FREIGHT
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Audit EPS foam usage. If you ship to California, New York, or any of the 8+ states with EPS bans, you need an alternative now — not when you get a compliance notice. Molded pulp and paper cushioning are the leading replacements. Pregis, Sealed Air, and Ranpak all offer EPS-free protective packaging.
EPS · FOAM BAN · COMPLIANCE
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Evaluate pallet pooling (CHEP, PECO, iGPS) vs. white wood purchase for your highest-volume shipping lanes. Pooling converts volatile pallet purchase costs to predictable per-trip fees, eliminates pallet storage/management, and provides consistent quality. Break-even is typically at 3-4 trips per pallet per year.
PALLETS · POOLING · CHEP · PECO
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Consolidate packaging suppliers. Most plants buy corrugated, stretch film, tape, and poly bags from 5-10 different suppliers. Consolidate to 2-3 (one corrugated manufacturer, one film/plastic supplier, one distributor for tail/specialty). Volume consolidation delivers better pricing and simplifies the supply chain.
CONSOLIDATION · VOLUME · SIMPLIFICATION
MONITOR
EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) legislation is expanding state by state. California SB 54 is the model — all packaging recyclable or compostable by 2032. If you ship to multiple states, track the regulatory landscape. Your packaging specifications may need to change for compliance, and the sooner you start transitioning, the lower the cost.
