HFC Refrigerant Phase-Down — AIM Act & Kigali AmendmentEPA · FEDERAL REGISTER
What's Happening
The American Innovation and Manufacturing (AIM) Act of 2020 mandates an 85% reduction in HFC production and consumption by 2036. The EPA implements this through declining annual quotas on HFC production and imports. Each year, less refrigerant is available — and prices rise accordingly. R-410A (the most common commercial refrigerant) has gone from $12/lb to $38/lb in 18 months. R-22 (already banned for new equipment) is $85/lb for reclaimed stock.

AIM Act Phase-Down Schedule
2024: 60% of baseline → 2029: 30% of baseline → 2034: 20% of baseline → 2036: 15% of baseline. Every step down reduces available HFC supply and increases prices. The 2029 step to 30% will be the most disruptive — it cuts current allocation in half. Plan your equipment transitions to be complete before 2029.
California Leading
CARB has already banned R-410A in new residential and light commercial AC systems as of January 2025. Other states (Washington, New York, Colorado, Maryland) are following. If you have facilities in these states, new equipment must use R-454B or equivalent NOW — not when the federal ban takes effect.

Cooling Tower Chemical TreatmentEPA · STATE DISCHARGE
Why This Matters
Cooling towers are the largest single water consumer in most industrial facilities. Chemical treatment (biocides, corrosion inhibitors, scale inhibitors, pH control) typically costs $50K-500K/year depending on system size. Most plants outsource this to a single provider — creating the same lock-in dynamics as OEM-spec lubricants. The treatment provider controls your water chemistry, and switching is perceived as risky. That perception costs you 20-30% in overspend.

Breaking the Lock-In
STEP 1
Get independent water analysis. Don't rely solely on your treatment provider's lab — they have an incentive to over-treat. Independent testing costs $500-1,000 and gives you an unbiased baseline.
STEP 2
Unbundle chemistry from service. Buy chemicals competitively and contract service separately. Bundled programs obscure markup — typical chemical markup in bundled contracts is 40-60%.
STEP 3
Run a competitive RFP. Nalco, ChemTreat, and Solenis will all compete aggressively for your business. The incumbent's best price is usually the one they offer when they're about to lose the account.
Ecolab / Nalco Dominance
Ecolab acquired Nalco in 2011 for $5.4B, creating the dominant water treatment company globally. Nalco Water is now Ecolab's largest business segment. If you also use Ecolab for pest control, food safety, or institutional cleaning — map your total Ecolab spend. You may have more leverage than you think.
Source: SEC EDGAR, Ecolab/Danaher annual reports, EPA discharge permits

Refrigerant Tariff + AIM Act = Double Hit
Chinese-origin HFC refrigerants face 25% Section 301 tariff on top of an already supply-constrained market (AIM Act quotas). Non-Chinese refrigerant sources (Chemours/US, Honeywell/US, Daikin/Japan) avoid the tariff. For refrigerant procurement, domestic supply is the only cost-effective path.
USMCA Manufacturing
All four major chiller OEMs (Trane, Carrier, York/JCI, Daikin) have manufacturing in the US and/or Mexico. Unlike many industrial categories, HVAC equipment is largely domestically produced. The tariff exposure is concentrated in components, refrigerants, and smaller imported split/VRF systems — not in the major chiller lines.
