Fleet Operating Cost Structure
FUEL — 30-40%
Directly tracks EIA gasoline/diesel. At $4.04/gal diesel and 15,000 miles/yr at 12 MPG (Class 3-5 trucks), fuel cost is $5,050/vehicle/year. On a 50-truck fleet, that is $252K/year in fuel alone. Every $0.50/gal increase adds $31K/year to fleet cost.
DEPRECIATION/LEASE — 25-30%
Vehicle acquisition is the second-largest cost. Lease (operating or capital) vs purchase vs reimbursement are fundamentally different financial structures. The optimal answer depends on fleet size, utilization, maintenance capability, and tax situation.
MAINTENANCE — 15-20%
Preventive + unscheduled. FMC maintenance networks (Element, ARI) negotiate fleet discounts with repair chains (Pep Boys, Firestone, dealer networks). Managing maintenance in-house requires a fleet maintenance program. The FMC's maintenance discount is a key lock-in mechanism — leaving them means losing the negotiated rates.
INSURANCE — 8-12%
Commercial auto insurance up 12-18% annually driven by nuclear verdicts and repair cost inflation. Telematics data (hard braking, speeding, idle time) can reduce premiums 5-15% by demonstrating driver safety. If you have telematics and your insurer is not giving you a discount, switch insurers.

Fuel Cost Management & HedgingEIA · NYMEX
Fuel Is Your Biggest Variable Cost
Crude at $98.71/bbl. Diesel at $4.04/gal. Gasoline at $3.48/gal. Hormuz disruption risk extends crude exposure indefinitely. On a 100-vehicle fleet averaging 15,000 miles/year at 15 MPG (mixed fleet), annual fuel spend is ~$350K at current prices. A 20% crude spike adds $70K to your annual fuel bill.
FUEL CARD OPTIMIZATION
WEX, FleetCor (FLEETCOR), Comdata, EFS, Fuelman — all offer fleet fuel cards with per-gallon rebates. Typical savings: $0.03-0.08/gallon based on volume. Some programs offer site-restriction to lowest-cost stations. Require a competitive bid from at least 3 fuel card providers and compare the all-in cost (transaction fee + rebate + reporting).
FUEL HEDGING (LARGE FLEETS)
For fleets consuming 500K+ gallons/year, fuel hedging (fixed-price contracts or NYMEX futures) can cap exposure. Hedge 50-70% of expected consumption at current prices, leave 30-50% floating to benefit from price drops. Work with a fuel broker (Mansfield Energy, Sprague, Papco) — they structure hedges for commercial fleets without requiring you to trade futures directly.
IDLE REDUCTION — 5-10% FUEL SAVINGS
Telematics data shows average fleet vehicles idle 20-30% of engine-on time. At idle, a Class 5 truck burns 0.8-1.2 gallons/hour. On a 50-truck fleet with 2 hours daily idle time, that is 25,000-37,000 gallons/year wasted — $85K-125K at current diesel prices. Anti-idle policies + telematics monitoring + driver coaching cuts idle time 40-60%.
RIGHT-SIZING VEHICLES
Many fleets spec vehicles larger than needed. A Class 5 truck (12 MPG) doing a job a Class 3 van (18 MPG) could handle wastes 33% more fuel per mile. For every 100 vehicles, spec optimization typically finds 15-25 that are oversized. Right-sizing saves fuel AND reduces acquisition/insurance cost.
ROUTE OPTIMIZATION
Route optimization software (Routific, OptimoRoute, Samsara) reduces miles driven 10-20% through sequencing, clustering, and real-time traffic routing. On 100 vehicles driving 15,000 miles/year, a 15% reduction eliminates 225,000 miles — saving 15,000+ gallons ($50K+) plus wear/maintenance. ROI: 3-6 months.
Source: EIA, NYMEX, DOE AFDC, fleet industry benchmarks
EV Transition — The Biggest Fleet Decision in 20 YearsIRA · DOE · EPA
The Math Has Changed
IRA commercial EV tax credits ($7,500 light-duty, up to $40,000 heavy-duty), falling battery costs (down 80% in 10 years), and $4+ diesel make EV fleet economics viable for many applications NOW — not in 5 years. A Ford F-150 Lightning Pro at $55K minus $7,500 credit = $47.5K. Fuel savings at 15,000 miles/year: ~$3,500/year vs diesel F-150. Maintenance savings: ~$1,500/year (no oil changes, less brake wear). Total annual savings: ~$5,000. Payback on the ICE-to-EV premium: 3-4 years.
WHERE EVs WIN TODAY
Return-to-base fleets: Vehicles that return to a depot nightly (delivery vans, service trucks, utility vehicles). Charge overnight on Level 2 chargers ($0.08-0.12/kWh off-peak vs $4.04/gal diesel). Urban/suburban routes under 150 miles/day. Light-duty applications. Amazon, FedEx, UPS, and USPS are all deploying tens of thousands of electric delivery vans for exactly these use cases.
WHERE EVs DO NOT WIN YET
Long-haul routes exceeding 200 miles/day. Heavy-duty applications requiring max payload. Battery weight reduces payload capacity 1,000-2,000 lbs on Class 5+ trucks. Remote locations without charging infrastructure. Extreme cold climates (range drops 20-40% below 20°F). For these applications, stay with ICE or evaluate plug-in hybrids as a bridge.
CHARGING INFRASTRUCTURE
Level 2 charger (240V, 19.2kW): $1,500-3,000 installed per port. Charges overnight (8-10 hours for full battery). For depot charging, Level 2 is sufficient and most cost-effective. DC fast charging ($30K-100K per unit) only needed for mid-day top-ups on high-utilization routes. Many utilities offer make-ready programs that cover 50-100% of electrical infrastructure cost for fleet EV charging. Check your utility.
IRA TAX CREDITS — USE THEM
30C — EV Charger Credit: 30% of charger + installation cost, up to $100K per property. 45W — Commercial Clean Vehicle Credit: Up to $7,500 (light-duty) or $40,000 (heavy-duty) per vehicle. Bonus depreciation: 80% first-year write-off on EV purchase price (2026). Stack all three: buy EV, install charger, take 30C + 45W + bonus depreciation. Effective cost reduction: 40-50% vs sticker price.
PILOT FIRST, THEN SCALE
Do not convert your entire fleet at once. Pilot 5-10 EVs on your best-fit routes (return-to-base, under 150 miles/day, Level 2 charging available). Run for 12 months. Measure actual: range in your conditions, charging cost, maintenance savings, driver feedback. Then scale based on data, not projections. Every fleet OEM (Ford Pro, GM BrightDrop, Rivian Commercial, Lightning eMotors) offers pilot programs.
Source: IRA Sections 30C/45W, DOE AFDC, EPA, EIA, fleet OEM data
