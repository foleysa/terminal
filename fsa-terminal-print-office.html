The Post-COVID Print Problem
Your Print Fleet Is Sized for 2019
Hybrid work permanently reduced office print volumes 30-50%. But most companies still have the same number of copiers, the same MPS contract minimums, and the same per-page rates negotiated when the office was at full capacity. A 100-person office that printed 200,000 pages/month in 2019 now prints 100,000-140,000 — but the MPS contract minimum is still 200,000 pages. You are paying for 60,000-100,000 pages you do not print. This is free money waiting to be recovered.
Office Supplies — The Amazon Effect
Amazon Business has disrupted traditional office supply contracts (Staples Business Advantage, Office Depot/ODP). Amazon's pricing on commodity items (pens, paper, folders, sticky notes) is typically 15-30% below contract pricing from traditional office supply companies. For most organizations, the optimal strategy is: contracted pricing on top 50 SKUs with a traditional supplier + Amazon Business for the long tail of commodity items.

Managed Print Services — Contract AuditFSA ANALYSIS
How MPS Contracts Work Against You
A Managed Print Services contract bundles hardware lease, maintenance, toner, and service into a single monthly payment — typically structured as a base charge (minimum pages) plus a per-page overage rate. The provider (Xerox, Ricoh, Canon, Konica Minolta) profits most when your actual volume is well below the minimum — you pay the base regardless. MPS contracts were sized in 2018-2019 when offices were full. Post-COVID, most organizations print 30-50% less but pay the same base. The provider has no incentive to tell you this.
MPS Traps
TRAP 1 — MINIMUM VOLUME COMMITMENT
Your contract has a monthly minimum page count (e.g., 200,000 pages). If you print 130,000, you still pay for 200,000. The base charge covers the minimum. This was fair when you actually printed 200,000. Now that you print 130,000, you are overpaying by 35%. Renegotiate the minimum to match actual volume — or negotiate a true cost-per-page model with no minimum.
TRAP 2 — AUTO-RENEWAL (3-5 YEARS)
Same as uniforms — MPS contracts auto-renew for the full original term (3-5 years) with 60-90 day cancellation windows. Miss it and you are locked in at the same oversized fleet and volume commitment. Calendar the date. File termination notice even if you plan to renew.
TRAP 3 — DEVICE COUNT FLOOR
Many MPS contracts have a minimum device count. You cannot remove devices below the floor without penalty. If your contract says 20 copiers minimum and you only need 12, you are paying for 8 devices that sit unused in empty conference rooms. Negotiate device flexibility in the next contract — the right to add or remove devices quarterly.
TRAP 4 — OEM TONER ONLY
MPS contracts require OEM toner (Xerox, Ricoh, Canon branded). Using compatible/remanufactured toner voids the service agreement. OEM toner costs 40-60% more than compatible alternatives. If you exit MPS and self-manage your fleet, compatible toner from LD Products, Clover Imaging, or Amazon cuts toner cost in half with comparable quality for most office printing.
How to Fix Your MPS
FIX 1 — RIGHT-SIZE THE FLEET
Pull 6 months of meter reads from every device. Any copier below 2,000 pages/month should be removed or replaced with a desktop printer. Consolidate to fewer, higher-utilization devices. Target: 1 MFP per 15-25 employees (post-COVID ratio), not the 1 per 8-10 that most offices still have.
FIX 2 — RESET THE MINIMUM
When renegotiating, set the minimum volume at 80% of your trailing 6-month average. If you averaged 130,000 pages/month, your new minimum should be 104,000 — not 200,000. Better yet, negotiate a pure cost-per-page model with zero minimum and a slightly higher per-page rate. You only pay for what you print.
FIX 3 — EVALUATE SELF-MANAGED
For companies with 10 or fewer devices, self-managed printing (buy or lease devices independently, use compatible toner, contract maintenance separately) is often 25-40% cheaper than MPS. MPS makes sense at scale (20+ devices, dedicated print room, high volume). Below that threshold, you are paying for management overhead you do not need.
FIX 4 — PRINT POLICIES
Implement default duplex (double-sided) printing, black-and-white default (color on request), and pull-printing (user authenticates at the device to release the job). Pull-printing alone reduces print volume 10-20% because jobs that are never picked up never print. These are software settings — zero cost, immediate savings.

⚠ Amazon Business — The Disruptor
Amazon Business is taking share from Staples and Office Depot on commodity office supplies. Pricing is 15-30% below contract rates on most items. Business Prime adds free shipping, approval workflows, and analytics. For organizations spending under $100K/year on office supplies, Amazon Business may eliminate the need for a traditional contract entirely. For larger programs, use Amazon pricing as your benchmark in Staples/ODP negotiations.
⚠ Compatible Toner Market
LD Products, Clover Imaging Group, V4INK, Ink E-Sale — all offer compatible/remanufactured toner cartridges at 40-60% below OEM pricing. Quality on modern compatibles is within 5% of OEM for standard office printing. The risk: using compatible toner voids your MPS service agreement. Only switch to compatible if you are self-managing your fleet or your MPS contract explicitly allows it.

Strategic Sourcing Actions — Print & Office SuppliesFSA INTELLIGENCE
URGENT — MPS CONTRACT REVIEW
Pull your MPS contract. Check: (1) monthly minimum page commitment vs actual volume, (2) auto-renewal date and cancellation window, (3) device count floor, (4) per-page rate for B&W and color. If actual volume is 30%+ below the minimum, you are overpaying immediately. File termination notice to force renegotiation even if renewal is months away.
MPS · MINIMUM · AUTO-RENEWAL · REVIEW
URGENT — FLEET COUNT
Pull meter reads for every printer and copier. Any device below 2,000 pages/month is a candidate for removal. Each removed MFP saves $200-500/month. Target fleet ratio: 1 MFP per 15-25 employees (post-COVID). If you still have 1 per 8-10, your fleet is 40-60% oversized. Remove devices, consolidate to fewer high-utilization units.
FLEET · RIGHT-SIZE · METER READS
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Implement print policies that cost nothing: default duplex (saves 20-30% on paper), default B&W (color on request saves 60-80% per page in toner), and pull-printing/secure print release (reduces waste prints 10-20%). These are configuration changes in your print server — no hardware or contract changes needed.
DUPLEX · B&W DEFAULT · PULL-PRINT
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Benchmark your office supply contract (Staples/ODP) against Amazon Business pricing on your top 50 SKUs by spend. Amazon is typically 15-30% cheaper on commodity items (paper, pens, toner, breakroom). Use the comparison as negotiation leverage — or split the business: contract for top 50, Amazon for the tail.
AMAZON · BENCHMARK · OFFICE SUPPLIES
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
If your fleet is under 10 devices, evaluate exiting MPS entirely. Buy or lease devices independently ($200-400/month each for quality MFPs via HP, Canon, or Kyocera dealers). Use compatible toner (40-60% savings). Contract maintenance separately or use break-fix. Self-managed printing at small scale is 25-40% cheaper than MPS.
SELF-MANAGED · COMPATIBLE TONER · EXIT MPS
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Audit breakroom supplies. Coffee, water delivery, snacks, and breakroom consumables are often on auto-replenishment at inflated pricing from a legacy vendor. Coffee services (Aramark, Canteen/Compass) typically charge 2-3x what direct-purchase from Costco or Amazon would cost. For a 100-person office, breakroom optimization saves $5K-15K/year.
BREAKROOM · COFFEE · AUTO-REPLENISHMENT
MONITOR
Print volume continues to decline 5-8% annually as digital workflows replace paper processes. Every year your fleet should get smaller, your minimum commitment should decrease, and your total print spend should decline. If your MPS spend is flat or increasing year-over-year while volume drops — the provider is extracting margin from a shrinking category. Push back at every renewal.
