Key DependenciesANALYSIS
Energy Is 40% of Gas Production Cost
Air separation (producing oxygen, nitrogen, argon from air) is extremely energy-intensive. Industrial gas prices track electricity costs directly. Regional electricity rates matter — a plant in BPA territory (Pacific NW, 4.8¢/kWh) pays less for nitrogen than a plant in ISO-NE (New England, 14.2¢/kWh). Same gas, different energy input cost.
Helium — Global Shortage
Helium is a non-renewable resource with limited global supply (primarily US, Qatar, Algeria, Russia). BLM's Federal Helium Reserve privatization, Qatar capacity constraints, and Russian sanctions have created persistent shortage. Helium prices up 6-10% annually for 5 consecutive years. If you use helium for TIG welding or leak detection, evaluate argon-helium blend alternatives to reduce pure helium consumption.
Distribution = 25% of Cost
Cylinder delivery is diesel-intensive (specialized hazmat trucks). At $4.04/gal diesel, delivery surcharges are escalating. For high-volume users, the answer is bulk delivery (liquid tank) instead of cylinders. Bulk pricing is 30-50% below cylinder pricing per unit of gas — and eliminates cylinder rental fees entirely.

⚠ Gas Oligopoly — Limited Leverage
Linde (35%), Air Liquide (25%), and Air Products (20%) control 80% of the US industrial gas market. After the Linde-Praxair merger in 2018, competition decreased further. Messer (formed from Linde-Praxair divestitures) is the only significant #4 player. For most geographies, you have 2-3 viable gas suppliers at best. The leverage play is bulk conversion (cylinders → liquid tank) and volume commitment — not switching suppliers, because there aren't many to switch to.
⚠ ITW / Miller / Hobart
Illinois Tool Works owns Miller Electric, Hobart, Bernard MIG guns, and Tregaskiss robotic torches. If your shop uses Miller welders, Hobart wire feeders, and Bernard consumables — that's one company across equipment, wire, and accessories. Consolidate the negotiation or use Lincoln Electric as the competitive alternative.

Cylinder Rental & Contract TrapsFSA ANALYSIS
The Cylinder Rental Problem
Industrial gas suppliers make as much margin on cylinder rental fees as they do on the gas itself. A typical industrial customer has 50-200 cylinders on site. At $3-8/month rental per cylinder, that's $1,800-19,200/year in rental fees alone — often exceeding the gas cost. Most customers accumulate cylinders over years without tracking returns. Empty cylinders sitting in the yard are generating rental revenue for the gas company every month.
Common Traps
TRAP 1 — DEMURRAGE ACCUMULATION
Cylinders that aren't returned within the "free days" (typically 30-60 days) start accruing demurrage charges on top of rental. A cylinder that sits empty for 6 months costs $30-60 in demurrage plus rental. Most plants have dozens of these.
TRAP 2 — TAKE-OR-PAY MINIMUMS
Bulk gas contracts often include minimum purchase volumes. If your production drops and you don't use the minimum, you still pay for it. A take-or-pay clause on a nitrogen tank at $0.15/ccf with 50,000 ccf/month minimum means $7,500/month regardless of usage. Negotiate usage-based pricing or lower minimums.
TRAP 3 — AUTO-RENEWAL
Most gas contracts auto-renew annually with 60-90 day cancellation windows. Miss the window and you're locked in for another year — at whatever rate increase the supplier applied. Calendar the cancellation window for every gas contract.
TRAP 4 — ENVIRONMENTAL/FUEL SURCHARGES
Gas suppliers have added environmental recovery fees ($5-15/delivery) and fuel surcharges (8-15%) on top of base pricing. These often appear after contract signing as "pass-through" charges not covered by the contracted gas price. Get all-in pricing in the contract — no surcharges.
How to Fix It
FIX 1 — CYLINDER AUDIT
Walk your yard and count every cylinder. Match against the gas company's inventory list. Identify empties, dormants, and wrong-supplier cylinders. Return everything you don't need this month. Most audits find 30-50% of on-site cylinders are unnecessary. Immediate rental savings.
FIX 2 — CONVERT TO BULK
If you use 10+ cylinders/month of any single gas, you're a candidate for bulk liquid delivery (microbulk or full bulk tank). Bulk pricing is 30-50% below cylinder pricing per unit. No cylinder rental. No demurrage. Fewer deliveries. The gas company installs the tank — your only cost is the gas.
FIX 3 — ON-SITE GENERATION
For high-volume nitrogen users (50,000+ scf/month), on-site nitrogen generators (PSA or membrane) produce nitrogen from compressed air at $0.02-0.05/ccf vs $0.12-0.20/ccf delivered. Payback: 12-24 months. After payback, your nitrogen is essentially free (just electricity). Parker, Atlas Copco, and Pneumatech all offer systems.
FIX 4 — NEGOTIATE ALL-IN
When rebidding gas supply, require all-in pricing: gas + delivery + rental + hazmat + environmental + fuel surcharge = one number per unit delivered. No line-item surprises. Compare all-in cost per ccf or per pound across suppliers — not just the base gas price.

Welding Wire — Triple Tariff
Chinese-origin welding wire faces Section 232 (steel, 25%), Section 301 (China, 25%), and antidumping duties. Combined rates can exceed 50%. Domestic wire (Lincoln Electric, Hobart) avoids all tariffs. For carbon steel MIG wire, domestic sourcing is almost always cheaper than Chinese after tariffs. Stainless wire is more nuanced — specialty alloys may require imported sources.
Industrial Gases — Domestic
Industrial gases (oxygen, nitrogen, argon, CO₂) are produced domestically by air separation plants. No meaningful import exposure and minimal tariff relevance. The cost drivers are energy (electricity for air separation) and distribution (diesel for truck delivery) — not trade policy.
