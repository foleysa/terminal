Key DependenciesANALYSIS
Diesel — 28% of Carrier Cost
EIA diesel at $4.04/gal, up 18% YTD driven by crude at $98.71/bbl. Fuel surcharges should offset this for carriers — but many surcharge tables are designed to generate profit at high diesel prices, not just cover cost. At 6 MPG and $4.04 diesel, actual fuel cost is $0.67/mile. If your surcharge exceeds $0.70/mile, the carrier is making money on fuel.
Driver Shortage — 80,000 and Growing
ATA estimates an 80,000-driver shortage, projected to reach 160,000 by 2030. Average driver age: 46. The pipeline isn't replacing retirements. This is structural — it drives wage inflation (7.2% YTD), limits carrier capacity, and gives carriers pricing leverage. Carriers with the best driver retention will deliver the most reliable service.
Insurance — The Silent Escalator
Trucking insurance premiums up 12.8% — driven by nuclear verdicts (jury awards over $10M in accident litigation). This cost gets baked into carrier rates and is non-negotiable for the carrier. It's a structural cost increase across the entire industry that won't reverse.

⚠ Yellow Corp Bankruptcy — LTL Capacity Lost
Yellow Corporation (formerly YRC Worldwide) filed Chapter 11 in July 2023 and liquidated. 30,000 employees, 12,000 trucks, 300 terminals — gone. Estimated 8-10% of national LTL capacity permanently removed from the market. Remaining carriers (ODFL, Saia, XPO, FedEx Freight) absorbed the volume and the pricing power. LTL rates will not return to pre-Yellow levels.
⚠ Knight-Swift / AAA Cooper Acquisition
Knight-Swift (largest TL carrier) acquired AAA Cooper Transportation, expanding into LTL. TL and LTL increasingly under the same corporate roofs. Knight-Swift, FedEx, and XPO now compete across both modes — consolidation reduces your leverage.
⚠ TForce = Former UPS Freight
TFI International acquired UPS Freight in 2021 and rebranded it TForce Freight. If your legacy UPS Freight contract rolled to TForce, the terms may have changed. Review your pricing agreement — TForce has been rationalizing unprofitable accounts post-acquisition.

Accessorial Fee Audit — The #1 Hidden Cost in FreightFSA ANALYSIS
The Problem
Accessorial charges (fees beyond base line-haul rate) typically account for 15-25% of total freight spend — and most shippers don't audit them. Carriers apply accessorials liberally because most shippers lack the visibility or resources to challenge them. The result: you're paying for detention, liftgate, residential delivery, reweigh, and reclassification charges that may not be legitimate — or that could be eliminated with operational changes.
Most Common (and Abused) Accessorials
DETENTION — $75-150/HR AFTER FREE TIME
Carrier charges for wait time at pickup/delivery beyond free time (usually 2 hours). This is legitimate — but carriers increasingly bill detention even when delays are within free time, or bill for time spent in a queue that's the carrier's scheduling problem. Audit every detention charge against driver check-in/check-out timestamps.
REWEIGH / RECLASSIFICATION — LTL
LTL carriers weigh and reclassify shipments at their terminals. If the actual weight or freight class differs from the BOL, they rebill at the higher rate. This is the single most abused accessorial in LTL. Invest in certified scales at your dock and train shipping staff on correct NMFC classification. Eliminate the carrier's reweigh revenue stream.
FUEL SURCHARGE — VERIFY THE MATH
Fuel surcharges are indexed to DOE diesel. But the surcharge table in your contract may not match actual carrier fuel consumption. At 6 MPG and $4.04 diesel, actual fuel cost is $0.67/mile. If your surcharge exceeds this, the carrier is profiting on fuel. Negotiate a transparent, MPG-based surcharge formula.
LIFTGATE / INSIDE DELIVERY
$75-150 per occurrence. Legitimate when required — but carriers sometimes bill liftgate on deliveries to facilities with dock-height loading docks. Verify that liftgate charges match actual delivery locations. If your facility has a dock, you shouldn't be paying for a liftgate.
How to Audit & Reduce
STEP 1 — PULL 12 MONTHS OF INVOICES
Categorize every charge by type: line-haul, fuel surcharge, detention, reweigh, reclassification, liftgate, residential, appointment, limited access. Calculate what percentage of total spend each accessorial represents. Most shippers are shocked when they see the total.
STEP 2 — CHALLENGE THE TOP 3
Take your top 3 accessorial categories by spend. Pull supporting documentation (BOLs, delivery receipts, timestamps, weight tickets). Challenge every charge that doesn't have clear supporting documentation. Carriers will credit 20-40% of challenged charges — they expect most shippers to never look.
STEP 3 — FIX THE ROOT CAUSE
Detention? Fix your dock scheduling to reduce wait times. Reweigh? Install certified scales and train on NMFC. Reclassification? Get your freight classification right at origin. Liftgate? Update your delivery location profile with the carrier. Every accessorial has an operational fix that eliminates it permanently.
STEP 4 — NEGOTIATE CAPS
In your next carrier contract, negotiate: detention free time minimum (3 hours, not 2), fuel surcharge cap (tied to actual MPG), reweigh tolerance (± 100 lbs before reclassification), and accessorial caps (max per shipment and per month). Get the caps in the contract — not in a side letter.

DOT / FMCSA Regulatory ImpactFEDERAL REGISTER · FMCSA
Active Regulations
ELD MANDATE — FULLY ENFORCED
Electronic Logging Devices required on all CMVs. Eliminates off-book driving hours. Reduces effective capacity by 3-5% vs pre-ELD era because drivers can no longer exceed HOS limits without detection. This capacity reduction is permanent and baked into the market.
HOURS OF SERVICE (HOS)
11-hour daily driving limit, 14-hour on-duty window, 30-minute break required after 8 hours. Determines how far a truck can go in a day (~550 miles max). Shippers with facilities in the middle of nowhere (more than 5 hours from major markets) face inherently higher costs because they burn a driver's daily hours on a single delivery.
CSA SCORES — CARRIER SAFETY
FMCSA Compliance, Safety, Accountability scores are public. Check your carriers' CSA BASICs scores at ai.fmcsa.dot.gov/SMS. Any carrier with an "Alert" status in Unsafe Driving or Crash Indicator categories is a liability risk. Your contract should require carriers to maintain satisfactory CSA scores — and give you termination rights if they don't.
Upcoming / Proposed
EPA GHG PHASE 3 — TRUCK EMISSIONS
EPA's Phase 3 greenhouse gas standards for heavy-duty trucks (model year 2027+) require significant NOx and CO2 reductions. Carriers will pass through the higher cost of compliant equipment. New truck prices increasing $10-20K per unit. Carriers that delay fleet renewal will have older, less fuel-efficient trucks — and higher fuel surcharges.
CARB ADVANCED CLEAN TRUCKS (CALIFORNIA)
California's ACT rule requires manufacturers to sell increasing percentages of zero-emission trucks starting 2024. By 2035, 55% of Class 8 tractors sold in CA must be zero-emission. If you have significant California freight, your carriers will need electric/hydrogen trucks — and the cost will be in your rates.
SPEED LIMITER MANDATE — PROPOSED
FMCSA proposed requiring speed limiters on all CMVs over 26,000 lbs. If enacted, trucks would be limited to 65-68 MPH. Reduces average transit speed, increases transit times, and effectively reduces capacity. Monitor Federal Register for final rule.
Source: FMCSA, Federal Register, EPA GHG Phase 3, CARB ACT rule

SAFETY · CSA · CARRIER QUALIFICATION
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Evaluate dedicated fleet vs. common carrier for your highest-volume, most consistent lanes. Dedicated fleet pricing is 10-15% higher than spot market — but you get guaranteed capacity, consistent drivers who know your facilities, and no accessorial surprises. For lanes running 5+ loads/week, the math often favors dedicated.
DEDICATED · CAPACITY · CONSISTENCY
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
For TL lanes over 500 miles, evaluate intermodal (rail + dray). J.B. Hunt, Schneider, and Hub Group all offer intermodal. Pricing is typically 10-20% below TL, but transit time is 1-2 days longer. For non-time-sensitive freight, intermodal saves money AND reduces your carbon footprint (rail is 4x more fuel-efficient than truck).
INTERMODAL · COST · SUSTAINABILITY
MONITOR
EPA GHG Phase 3 and California ACT rule will increase carrier equipment costs 15-25% over the next 5 years. This gets passed through in rates. Build escalation assumptions into your 3-year freight budget. Carriers that invest early in compliant equipment will have higher near-term costs but better long-term positioning.
