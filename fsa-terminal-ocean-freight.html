Why This Is Structural, Not TemporaryANALYSIS
Rerouting Distance
Cape of Good Hope adds 3,000-4,000 nautical miles per voyage. Asia-to-Europe transit: 30 days → 44 days. Every round trip ties up a ship for 2 extra weeks.
Effective Capacity Reduction
Longer voyages = fewer round trips per ship per year. The global fleet hasn't grown, but the route network just got 15-20% longer. That's a structural capacity squeeze.
Compounding Costs
Fuel, insurance, and port fees are stacking simultaneously. Gulf transit insurance premiums up 300%. Bunker fuel consumption rises with every extra mile. These don't unwind when headlines fade.
Forecast
Expect 2-3 years of elevated shipping costs minimum. New vessel deliveries in 2027-2028 will help — but they were ordered for pre-Hormuz route networks, not post-rerouting capacity needs.
Active Disruptions — Global ShippingLIVE MONITORING
Strait of Hormuz — Closure / Restricted Transit
STATUS: CRITICAL · SINCE FEB 2026 · DURATION: INDEFINITE
Iranian naval exercises have effectively closed the Strait to commercial tanker traffic. 20% of global oil supply disrupted. Container vessels rerouting around Cape of Good Hope, adding 14+ days to Asia-Europe and Asia-US East Coast routes.
⚡ Impact: +$200-400/TEU surcharge (now permanent), +14 days transit, bunker fuel costs up 28%, war risk insurance up 300%.
Maersk Permanent Surcharge Restructuring
STATUS: HIGH · EFFECTIVE MAR 2026 · PERMANENT
Maersk has made the $200/TEU and $400/FEU emergency Gulf surcharge permanent through 2027. Hapag-Lloyd, CMA CGM, and MSC have followed with similar structures. This is not a temporary premium — it's the new baseline.
⚡ Impact: All annual contracts signed pre-February are below market. Carriers will degrade service quality on unprofitable lanes before they eat the loss.
Singapore / Rotterdam Port Congestion
STATUS: ELEVATED · SINCE MAR 2026 · WORSENING
Cape rerouting is concentrating vessel arrivals at Singapore (transshipment hub) and Rotterdam (Europe gateway). Both ports reporting 3-5 day berthing delays. Container dwell times up 40%.
⚡ Impact: Add 3-5 days to transit estimates on routes through Singapore or Rotterdam. Book early or accept delays.
US West Coast Labor — ILWU Contract Expiration
STATUS: MONITOR · CONTRACT EXPIRES JUL 2026
ILWU contract covering West Coast ports expires July 2026. Negotiations haven't started. The 2022 contract negotiations caused months of disruption. If talks stall, expect cargo diversion to East and Gulf Coast ports.
⚡ Impact: If you route through LA/Long Beach, Oakland, or Seattle-Tacoma — have contingency routes planned through Savannah, Houston, or Charleston by June.
Sources: Lloyd's List, Drewry, Journal of Commerce, USMX/ILWU public filings

⚠ Alliance Concentration Risk
Three alliances (Gemini, Ocean Alliance, Premier Alliance) plus MSC independently control 80%+ of global container capacity. If you're 80% with one carrier, you have no leverage. Split volume across 3+ carriers minimum — and across at least 2 alliances.
⚠ Gemini Alliance — New in 2025
Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd launched the Gemini alliance in February 2025, replacing 2M. Early schedule reliability is the lowest in the market at 64-68%. If reliability matters more than price, shift volume toward Evergreen or COSCO (75-76% on-time).
Source: Alphaliner fleet data, Drewry schedule reliability index, carrier public surcharge schedules

Nearshore Advantage
Intra-Americas routes (Mexico, Central America, Colombia, Brazil) are the least disrupted and lowest cost. For any category currently sourced from Asia, run a total landed cost comparison using current freight rates. The nearshore math has flipped in many categories — especially for goods with a high freight-to-value ratio.
Panama Canal Restrictions
Panama Canal continues to restrict daily transits due to drought conditions. Asia → US East Coast routing via Panama is unreliable. Most carriers are routing via Cape or Suez (where accessible). Factor this into US East Coast sourcing strategies.

Strategic Sourcing Actions — Ocean FreightFSA INTELLIGENCE
URGENT — ACT THIS WEEK
Any annual container contract signed before February 2026 is now 30-50% below market. Carriers will degrade service quality before they absorb the loss. Renegotiate terms NOW — push for index-linked rates (tied to Drewry WCI or Freightos FBX) with quarterly true-ups instead of fixed annual pricing.
CONTRACTS · CARRIER RELATIONS · PRICING
URGENT — ACT THIS WEEK
Recalculate ALL safety stock for ocean-shipped goods. Pre-Hormuz lead times are wrong. Add minimum 14 days to Asia-Europe and 7 days to Asia-US transit times. The cost of carrying extra inventory is less than the cost of a stockout.
INVENTORY · LEAD TIME · PLANNING
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Diversify carriers across alliances. If you're 80%+ with one carrier, you have zero leverage. Split volume across 3+ carriers minimum, spanning at least 2 of the 3 alliances (Gemini, Ocean Alliance, Premier Alliance). Concentration in logistics is as dangerous as in your supply base.
CARRIER DIVERSIFICATION · LEVERAGE
STRATEGIC — 30 DAYS
Run total landed cost comparison: Asia-sourced vs nearshore for every high-volume category. Include current freight ($4,800-6,200/FEU), transit time cost of capital, safety stock carrying cost, and tariff exposure. Mexico and Central America are now cost-competitive in categories where they weren't 6 months ago.
NEARSHORE · TOTAL LANDED COST · SOURCING
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
ILWU West Coast port contract expires July 2026. If you route significant volume through LA/Long Beach, Oakland, or Seattle-Tacoma — develop contingency plans to divert to Savannah, Houston, or Charleston by June. Don't wait for the headlines.
LABOR RISK · PORT DIVERSIFICATION
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Evaluate schedule reliability as a procurement criterion, not just rate. Gemini alliance (Maersk/Hapag-Lloyd) currently has the lowest on-time performance at 64-68%. If your supply chain can't absorb variability, pay the premium for Evergreen or COSCO (75-76% reliability) even if the rate is slightly higher.
RELIABILITY · CARRIER SELECTION
MONITOR
Watch for new vessel deliveries in 2027-2028. The current order book is significant — but vessels were ordered for pre-Hormuz routes. If Hormuz reopens AND new capacity arrives simultaneously, rates could correct sharply. Build contract flexibility to capture that downside. Don't lock into 3-year fixed rates at today's peaks.
