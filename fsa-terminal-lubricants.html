Base Oil Groups — Crude SensitivityANALYSIS
Group I — Highest Crude Exposure
Traditional solvent-refined base oil. Cheapest per gallon but most sensitive to crude price swings. Used in industrial gear oils, hydraulic fluids, and general-purpose lubricants. Price tracks crude almost 1:1.
Group II/III — Moderate Exposure
Hydrocracked and severely hydrocracked base oils. Better performance, less crude-sensitive than Group I but still petroleum-derived. The mainstream choice for engine oils and premium industrial lubricants. 60-80% crude correlation.
Group IV (PAO) / Group V — Lowest Exposure
Synthetic base oils. Manufactured from ethylene (PAO) or specialty chemistry (esters, PAG). Less tied to crude pricing — driven by chemical feedstock costs instead. Premium priced but most stable during crude volatility. The hedge for your most critical equipment.

OEM Specification Lock-In — The Hidden Cost DriverFSA ANALYSIS
The Problem
Equipment OEMs specify their own branded lubricants ("use only Mobil SHC 600 Series" or "approved: Shell Tellus S3 M only"). Maintenance teams comply without questioning whether equivalent-spec alternatives exist. The result: you're locked into a single lubricant supplier for the life of the equipment, with zero negotiating leverage during price escalations.
Common OEM Spec Locks
TYPICAL LOCK-IN
Compressors: Atlas Copco specifies Roto-Inject — a Shell-blended product. Alternatives exist at ISO VG46/68 PAO synthetic that meet or exceed the spec at 30% lower cost.
TYPICAL LOCK-IN
Hydraulics: Equipment manual says "Mobil DTE 10 Excel Series." The actual spec is ISO VG32/46 anti-wear hydraulic fluid. Dozens of suppliers make equivalent products.
TYPICAL LOCK-IN
Gearboxes: OEM says "Shell Omala S4 GXV only." The spec is ISO VG220/320 PAG synthetic gear oil. Fuchs, Klüber, and Quaker Houghton all make equivalent formulations.
How to Break the Lock
STEP 1
Identify the ACTUAL performance specification behind the brand name. ISO viscosity grade, additive type (AW, EP, R&O), base oil group, and any industry standards (API, AGMA, DIN).
STEP 2
Request cross-reference from 2-3 independent lubricant suppliers (Fuchs, Quaker Houghton, Castrol, Citgo). They will map their equivalent product to the OEM spec.
STEP 3
Run a side-by-side field trial on non-critical equipment. 90 days with oil analysis. If the alternative meets or exceeds performance, you've just created competitive leverage.
STEP 4
Use the approved alternative as leverage. Even if you stay with the OEM brand, the fact that you have a qualified alternative changes the pricing negotiation entirely.
Typical Savings
Breaking OEM lubricant lock-in typically saves 25-40% on lubricant spend without any performance degradation. For a facility spending $500K/year on lubricants, that's $125K-200K in annual savings — every year, recurring. The ROI on a 90-day field trial is measured in weeks, not months.
Source: FSA analysis, STLE lubrication best practices, independent tribology studies

Low Tariff Category Overall
Lubricants face relatively low tariffs compared to other industrial categories — the MFN duty is minimal (5.25¢/bbl). Even the 301 tariff on Chinese-origin lubricants is less impactful here because most major lubricant suppliers (Shell, ExxonMobil, Chevron, Total) blend domestically with US Gulf Coast base oils. The real cost driver is crude price, not tariffs.

SYNTHETIC · TCO · HEDGING
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Implement oil analysis on your top 20 pieces of rotating equipment. Oil analysis costs $15-25 per sample and tells you when oil actually needs changing (condition-based) vs changing on a fixed schedule (time-based). Most plants change oil too frequently — condition-based maintenance can reduce lubricant consumption 20-40%.
OIL ANALYSIS · CONDITION-BASED · SAVINGS
STRATEGIC — NEXT QUARTER
Consolidate lubricant suppliers. Most industrial facilities buy from 5-8 different lubricant brands because each piece of equipment was spec'd independently. Consolidating to 2 suppliers (one integrated major + one independent specialty) gives you volume leverage, simplifies inventory, and reduces the number of SKUs in your storeroom by 30-50%.
