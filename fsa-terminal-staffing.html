The Hidden Cost of Temp Labor
You Are Paying 35-55% Over What the Worker Earns
When you pay a staffing agency $28/hour for a production worker, the worker receives $18-20/hour. The rest ($8-10/hour) is the agency's markup covering: employer payroll taxes (7.65% FICA), workers comp insurance (5-15% for industrial), unemployment insurance (2-5%), ACA compliance, recruiter overhead, and profit. At 55% markup on a $20/hr worker, the bill rate is $31/hr. At 35% markup, it is $27/hr. That $4/hr difference on 100 temps working 2,000 hours/year = $800K/year.
Turnover Is the Real Expense
Industrial temp turnover runs 100-300% annually. Every turnover event costs $3,000-5,000 (recruiting, onboarding, training, lost productivity during ramp-up). On a 100-person temp workforce with 150% turnover, you are spending $450K-750K/year on turnover alone — often more than the staffing markup. Reducing turnover from 150% to 100% saves $150K-250K/year. The solution is pay rate competitiveness (raise the worker's pay by $1-2/hr and capture it back through lower markup and reduced turnover).

Markup Transparency — What You Are Actually Paying ForFSA ANALYSIS · BLS
Markup Components
EMPLOYER PAYROLL TAXES — 7.65%
FICA (Social Security 6.2% + Medicare 1.45%). Non-negotiable. Every employer pays this. If the agency claims payroll tax is 10%+ of the bill rate, they are padding.
WORKERS COMP INSURANCE — 5-15%
Varies dramatically by classification code. A warehouse worker (NCCI code 8018) runs 5-8%. A steelworker (5057) runs 12-20%. Ask for the workers comp rate by classification code. If the agency is blending all workers into one high rate, they are overcharging on lower-risk positions.
UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE — 2-5%
State unemployment tax (SUTA) varies by state and employer experience rating. A well-managed staffing agency with low claims history has a SUTA rate of 1-3%. An agency with high turnover and frequent claims may be at 5%+ — and you are paying for their poor management through a higher markup.
ACA COMPLIANCE — 2-4%
Affordable Care Act requires employers to offer health coverage to full-time employees (30+ hours/week). The agency carries this cost — typically $2-4/hour for qualifying temps. Verify: is the agency actually offering ACA-compliant coverage, or just building in the cost and pocketing it?
RECRUITER OVERHEAD + PROFIT — 8-15%
This is the negotiable portion. Everything above is pass-through cost (taxes, insurance). The agency's recruiter salary, office overhead, technology, and profit margin are 8-15% of the bill rate. A well-run agency makes 6-8% net margin. If total markup exceeds 45%, their overhead/profit is excessive.
Target Markups by Category
Benchmark Markup Ranges
Light industrial / warehouse: 30-40% (target 30-35%)
Manufacturing / production: 35-45% (target 32-38%)
Skilled trades (welders, electricians): 25-35% (target 25-30%)
Professional / technical: 25-35% (target 22-28%)
Clerical / admin: 40-55% (target 35-42%)

If your current markup exceeds the upper range — you have immediate negotiation opportunity. If it falls below the target — verify the agency is paying competitive wages and carrying proper insurance.
VMS — The Visibility Tool
A Vendor Management System (VMS) like SAP Fieldglass, Beeline, or Coupa Contingent Workforce forces markup transparency across all staffing providers. Every order, fill, timesheet, and invoice is tracked. You see the pay rate and the bill rate separately. VMS pays for itself on programs over $5M/year in temp spend through markup visibility alone. Below $5M, a simple spreadsheet tracking pay rate vs bill rate by provider achieves 80% of the visibility at zero cost.
Temp-to-Hire Conversion Fee
Most staffing contracts include a conversion (temp-to-hire) fee of 15-25% of the worker's annual salary — or a minimum hours requirement (typically 520-720 hours) after which the fee is waived. If you are hiring 30%+ of your temps permanently, negotiate the conversion terms upfront: lower the percentage, reduce the minimum hours, or negotiate a declining fee schedule (e.g., 20% at 0 hours, 10% at 520 hours, 0% at 720 hours).
