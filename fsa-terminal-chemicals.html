⚠ Distribution Consolidation — Brenntag / Univar
Brenntag acquired Univar in 2023 for $6.5B, creating the dominant chemical distributor in North America. If you buy from both Brenntag and Univar, you now have one supplier. This merger reduced distribution competition significantly — leverage your volume for better terms, because there are fewer alternatives.
⚠ Dow / DuPont Legacy
DowDuPont merged in 2017 then split into three in 2019: Dow (materials science), DuPont (specialty), Corteva (agriculture). Despite the split, shared legacy sites and overlapping supply chains mean a disruption at a shared Dow/DuPont site can affect both companies' output. Map your exposure across all three entities.

PFAS — The Biggest Regulatory Risk in Chemicals
EPA is systematically restricting PFAS ("forever chemicals") across all applications. If any of your chemical inputs contain PFAS — including surfactants, coatings additives, lubricants, and fire suppressants — you need a reformulation plan. Several states have already banned PFAS in specific applications. This is accelerating, not slowing down.
Source: EPA TSCA inventory, Federal Register, OSHA standards, DOT/PHMSA HM-215P

Sourcing Advantage
Canada and Mexico are the tariff-advantaged sources for commodity chemicals under USMCA. German and Japanese specialty chemicals enter at low MFN rates (3-6%). Chinese-origin chemicals face 25%+ combined duty. For commodity chemicals (caustic soda, soda ash, basic acids), USMCA sources are the obvious choice on total cost.
